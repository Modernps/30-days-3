Account Management Challenge

Why is Managing your Google account Important?

Managing your Google Account is essential for security, privacy, and efficient access to services like Gmail and Drive. It protects data with features like Two-Factor Authentication, ensures backups, and customizes your experience. It also supports collaboration with tools like Google Workspace and offers parental controls for safe online activity.

Risks of Poor Google account Management

  • Data Breaches: Weak passwords or lack of Two-Factor Authentication can allow hackers to access sensitive information like emails, contacts, and financial data.
  • Identify Theft: Compromised accounts can lead to identify theft, as attackers gain access to personal details.
  • Loss of Data: Without proper backup settings, important files, photos, and documents can be permanently lsot in case of account issues or breaches.
  • Privacy Violation:Failing to manage privacy settings can expose your location, search history and personal data to advertisers or third parties.
  • Account Lockout:Without recovery options, losing access to your account becomes harder to resolve if you forgot your password or get hacked.
  • Compromised Devices:A hacked Google account can lead to unauthorized access to connected devices and synced apps, putting them at risk too.

Challenge Steps

Task:Create a strong password

Steps to Follow:

  • Go to Google Account Security: Navigate to Google Account Security and select "Password" under the "Signing in to Google" section.
  • Use at Least 12 Characters: Create a password with a minimum of 12 characters, mixing uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Avoid Common Words or Phrases: Don't use easily guessable words or personal information like your name or birthdate.
  • Use Random Combinations: Combine random letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Avoid Reusing Passwords: Use different passwords for each account to reduce the risk of a security breach.
  • Update Regularly: Change your passeword periodically to maintain security, especially after any security alerts or breaches.
    • Consider a Password Manager: To ensure uniqueness and complexity for every account, use password manager to generate and store passwords securely.

Task: Enable Two Factor Authentication(2FA)

Steps to Follow:

  • Go to Google Account Security: Visit Google Account Security
  • Select "2-Step Verification": Under the "Signing in to Google" section, click om "2-Step Verfication"
  • Click "Get Started": You may need to log in again for security purposes
  • Choose between: Google Prompts to recieve a prompt for confirmation, Authenticator App like Google Authenticator or Authy, SMS or Call to get verification codes via text or call.
  • Follow the Setup Instructions
  • Backup Options: Add a Backup method, such as another phone number, in case you lose access to your primary device.

Task: Check your Accounts Security page

Steps to Follow:

  • Sign into your Google account
  • Go the Security Section: On the left menu, click on "Security"
  • Review "Recent Security Activity": Under the "Recent Security events" section,check for any suspicious or unauthorised activity like a new device or password change.
  • Manage Device: Scroll down to "Your device" and review the list of devices current,y signed in to your Google account. Remove any that you don't recognize or no longer use.
  • Check Third-Party Access: In the "Third-party apps with account access" section, review which appps have access to your account.
  • Review Security Recommendation: At the top of the security tips or alerts and follow the recommended steps.
  • Set Up Alerts: Ensure Email notifications are enabled so you're alerted to any unusual sign-in attempts or security events.

Task: Update Recovery Options

Steps to Follow:

  • Sign into your Google Account.
  • Navigate to the Security Section: On the left sidebar, click on "Security"
  • Scroll to "Ways We can Verify It's You": In the "How you sign in to Google" section, look for the "Ways we can verify it's you" option, where you'll see your recovery email and phone number.
  • Update Recovery Email: Click on "Recovery email", Enter your current password if prompted, Add or change your recovery email address and click "Save".
  • Update Recovery Phone Number: Click on "Recovery phone", Enter your current password if prompted, Add or update your recovery phone number and verify it with the code sent to the new number.
  • Review and Confirm: After Updating, ensure the correct details are saved and verified.
  • Follow the prompts to complete the setup.

Task: Back your Important Data

Steps to Follow:

  • Go to Google Takeout: Visit Google Takeout
  • Select Data to Backup: Google provides options to export data from services like Gmail, Google Drive, Calender. By Default, all services are selected. You can deselect any data you don't want to include.
  • Choose Export Type: Scroll down and click "Next steps". Choose whether you want a one time or scheduled backups.
  • Select File Type & Delivery Method: Choose the file type (e.g., ZIP) and how you want to receive the backup (e.g., download link via email or directly to Google Drive).
  • Choose your alert preferences (email or phone notifications).
  • Create Export: Click “Create export” and Google will begin backing up your data. Depending on the amount, it may take some time, and you'll receive a notification when it’s ready for download.
  • Backup Specific Data: Use Google Drive to backup important files, Use Google Photos to automatically backup photos and videos, Use Gmail to backup important emails automatically in Gmail.

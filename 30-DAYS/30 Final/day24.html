Wave of Digital Payments

Manage and Secure Online Payment Methods

With over 220 million digital payment users in India and online transactions growing at a rate of 55% annually, payment scams have also surged. In 2022 alone, digital fraud cases rose by 19%, affecting millions of users across methods like UPI, credit cards, and bank transfers. Understanding the most common scams and how to avoid them is crucial for staying secure in the rapidly expanding digital economy.

Ways to secure your online transactions

UPI provides many benefits that make it a crucial part of India's digital payment ecosystem:

  • Use Strong Passwords: Ensure all payment accounts are protected by complex, unique passwords.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Add an extra layer of security with 2FA for payment accounts.
  • Monitor Transactions Regularly: Check for unauthorized or suspicious activity frequently.
  • Avoid Public Wi-Fi:Do not make payments over public, unsecured networks.

Common Payment Scams in India and How to Avoid Them

Online payment methods in India, like bank transfers, credit cards, e-wallets, and digital platforms, are convenient but also susceptible to various scams. Here's how scammers exploit each method and how to avoid falling victim.

Skimming:Fraudsters clone your card using skimming devices at ATMs or POS terminals, and use the information for fraudulent transactions.
Phishing Websites: Scammers create fake websites to steal card details when you enter them for online payments.
Card Not Present (CNP) Fraud: Fraudsters use stolen card information for online or phone purchases without physically needing your card.
Fraudulent Payment Requests:Scammers pose as sellers or buyers and trick you into approving false payment requests on platforms like Paytm or Google Pay.
Impersonation: Fraudsters pose as customer service agents and ask for your wallet PIN or OTP to access your account.
Fake Cashback Offers: You receive messages offering fake cashback, asking you to enter your wallet details, which leads to theft.
Phishing: Scammers impersonate banks or merchants and ask for your bank login credentials or OTP, leading to unauthorized transfers.
Vishing: Fraudsters call pretending to be from the bank, asking for account details or OTP for “verification” purposes.
Fake UPI/IMPS Links:Fraudsters send malicious links asking for payment through UPI or IMPS, which leads to unauthorized debits.

Cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, Ethereum, and others are decentralized digital currencies that enable direct transfers between users without needing a bank or intermediary.

Fake Investment Schemes: Scammers lure people into fraudulent crypto investment platforms, promising high returns and then disappearing with the funds.
Phishing for Wallet Keys: Fraudsters send fake emails or websites asking for your private wallet keys, leading to theft of your cryptocurrency.
Ponzi or Pyramid Schemes: Scammers run Ponzi schemes where they promise returns from “investing” in new cryptocurrencies.

Conclusion

Payment scams in India are becoming more sophisticated, targeting various online methods. To protect yourself, stay alert, use secure platforms, enable two-factor authentication, and regularly monitor your transactions. Being aware of common scams and following these simple steps can help you avoid fraud and ensure safer online payments.

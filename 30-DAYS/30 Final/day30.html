Defend and Prevent

Learn How to Report a Cybercrime and Steps to Take If You’re a Victim

As cybercrimes become more prevalent, it's crucial to know how to report them and what steps to take if you fall victim to one. Cybercrimes can range from phishing attacks and identity theft to ransomware and data breaches. These attacks can have severe financial and emotional consequences, but taking swift action and knowing how to report the crime can mitigate further damage and increase the chances of recovery.

Real-Life Incident: The 2017 Equifax Breach

One of the largest data breaches in history, the Equifax breach in 2017, exposed the personal information of over 147 million people. Hackers accessed names, Social Security numbers, birth dates, and credit card information. Victims who didn’t take action or report the incident faced increased risks of identity theft and fraud.


While the company faced legal consequences and set up a claims process for victims, many people weren’t aware of their rights or the steps to take to protect themselves. This incident highlighted the importance of reporting cybercrimes and the need for individuals to actively protect their identity in the aftermath of such breaches.

Importance of Reporting Cybercrimes

  • Legal Protection: Reporting a cybercrime ensures that your case is documented and that you have a legal record of the incident, which is essential for pursuing any action or recovery.

  • Prevention: By reporting, you help law enforcement identify and address patterns of cybercrime, which can lead to the prevention of future incidents.

  • Awareness: It also raises awareness about ongoing scams and helps authorities warn other potential victims.

  • Financial Recovery: In some cases, reporting a cybercrime may help in recovering lost funds or protecting financial assets.

  • Improved Cybersecurity: Law enforcement agencies often use reported cases to improve public security measures, helping to strengthen national and global cybersecurity defenses.

How to Avoid Report Different Cybercrimes

Phishing attacks involve fraudulently obtaining sensitive information like login credentials or financial data by impersonating a legitimate entity.

    1. Document the Attack:

  • Take screenshots of the phishing email, website, or SMS.
  • Save the phishing message without clicking on any links.

    • 2. Report to Government Agencies:

  • Report phishing emails to cybercrime.gov.in (the Government of India’s Cyber Crime Reporting Portal).
  • You can also report phishing attacks by sending an email to cert-in.org.in/, which is monitored by the Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In).

    • 3. Notify Your Bank or Financial Institution:

  • If the phishing attack targets your financial information, immediately inform your bank and follow their procedures to secure your account.
  • In case of any financial fraud, also report it to the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal at cybercrime.gov.in.

    4. Notify Financial Institutions:

  • Inform your bank, credit card providers, and any affected organizations to freeze or secure your accounts.

Identity theft involves someone using your personal information, like your name, PAN number, or Aadhaar card details, for fraudulent purposes.

    1. Document the Incident:

  • Keep records of suspicious activities, unauthorized transactions, or changes to your accounts.

    • 2. Report to Local Authorities:

  • File a report at the local police station. Indian law enforcement is required to file an FIR for identity theft under the Information Technology Act.

    • 3. Report to the Government Cybercrime Portal:

  • Go to the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in.) and file a complaint under the Identity Theft section.

    • 4. Notify Financial Institutions:

  • Inform your bank, credit card providers, and any affected organizations to freeze or secure your accounts.

    • 5. Credit Bureau Notification:

  • Contact CIBIL or other credit rating agencies to report identity theft and monitor your credit for any unusual activity.

Ransomware is a type of malware that locks users out of their systems or encrypts data, demanding payment to restore access.

    1. Isolate the Infected Device:

  • Disconnect the infected system from the internet and any networks to stop the ransomware from spreading.

    • 2. Document the Attack:

  • Take screenshots of any ransom messages or notifications. Keep records of the compromised data.

    • 3. Report to CERT-In:

  • Immediately report ransomware incidents to the Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In) by emailing incident@cert-in.org.in or visiting cert-in.org.in.

    • 4. Report to Cyber Crime Police:

  • File a complaint through the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal at cybercrime.gov.in. Ransomware attacks can also be reported at your nearest cybercrime cell.

    • 5. Seek Professional Help:

  • Consult a cybersecurity expert to assess the damage and prevent future attacks. Do not pay the ransom, as there’s no guarantee your files will be restored.

    • 6. Avoid Paying the Ransom:

  • Paying the ransom is discouraged. Restore your systems from backups if available and seek legal help to handle the situation.

A data breach occurs when unauthorized individuals gain access to sensitive information stored by an organization, such as customer details, personal records, or financial data.

    1. Identify the Breach:

  • Determine the extent of the data breach and document what information has been exposed.

    • 2. Report to CERT-In:

  • Data breaches should be reported to CERT-In (Indian Computer Emergency Response Team) at cert-in.org.in.
  • CERT-In is the nodal agency responsible for responding to cybersecurity incidents in India.

    • 3. Notify Affected Parties:

  • If you are responsible for an organization, notify affected customers or stakeholders about the breach and steps being taken to secure their data.

    • 4. Report to the Cyber Crime Portal:

  • If the data breach is personal or involves sensitive personal information, file a complaint with the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in.).

    • 5. Engage Legal and Cybersecurity Experts:

  • Engage legal professionals and cybersecurity experts to mitigate the breach and prevent future occurrences. Ensure compliance with India’s data protection regulations.

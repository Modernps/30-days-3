Day 9

Audit Challenge

Why Regularly Auditing Your Network is Essential

In today's digital landscape, our devices are more connected than ever, making it critical to regularly audit the devices connected to your network. Unauthorized or unfamiliar devices can pose serious security threats, and disconnecting them immediately is your first line of defense.

Real-Life Incident

In 2020, a major retailer suffered a data breach after attackers gained access through an unsecured smart device connected to their network. The breach exposed thousands of customer records and highlighted the importance of auditing devices regularly to prevent unauthorized access.

Risks of Not Auditing Your Network

Failing to audit your network can expose you to significant risks, such as:

  • Unauthorized access to personal and sensitive information.
  • Compromised devices that can be used to launch cyberattacks.
  • Slow network performance due to unwanted devices consuming bandwidth.
  • Risk of being part of a botnet, where your devices are used for malicious purposes.

Challenge Steps

Instructions: Access your router’s settings by typing its IP address into your browser's URL bar. From there, you can see all connected devices.

Instructions: Review the list of devices. Match each device with something you or someone in your home or office uses. Note any unfamiliar devices.

Instructions: If you see any devices that you don’t recognize, disconnect them immediately and change your Wi-Fi password to secure your network.

Instructions: Ensure each device connected to your network has strong security settings in place, such as updated software and strong passwords.

Instructions: Make it a habit to audit your network at least once a month. Use network monitoring tools to get alerts when unfamiliar devices connect to your network.

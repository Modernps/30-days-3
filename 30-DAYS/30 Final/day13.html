Day 13

Enhance Security & Improve Link Sharing

Why Shorten your Links

  • Short URLs look clean, professional, and easier to read.
  • Easier Sharing: Short links are ideal for platforms with character limits, like social media or messaging apps.
  • Tracking & Analytics: Most URL shorteners offer tracking, allowing you to measure clicks, engagement, and user behavior.
  • Custom Branding: Many services let you create branded short links, building trust with your audience.
  • Prevent Link Breakage: Long URLs are prone to breaking when pasted into emails or messages. Short links maintain functionality.

Real-Life Incident

In the 2012 Obama re-election campaign, shortened links helped track user engagement and optimize outreach. Clean, shareable URLs improved professionalism and saved space on platforms like Twitter. The data gathered from shortened links allowed real-time strategy adjustments, boosting engagement and contributing to the campaign's success.

Risks of Not shortening links

Failing to update your software can expose you to various risks, including:

  • Suspicion: Long Complicated URLs can appear untrustworthy to users.
  • Long URLs take up too much valuable space on platform.
  • Without Shortening, you lose the opportunity to gather insights on your links performance.
  • Users may avoid clicking on confusing or lengthy links.

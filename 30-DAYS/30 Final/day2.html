Day 2

Password Strength Challenge

Why Strong Passwords are Essential

In a world where cyber threats are ever-present, having strong passwords is crucial for protecting your personal and financial information. Weak passwords can lead to unauthorized access and identity theft. A strong password is your first line of defense against cyber attacks.

Real-Life Incident

In 2019, the data breach of a major social media platform exposed millions of user passwords that were stored in plain text. This incident underlined the necessity of using strong, unique passwords for each account to safeguard against such breaches. The breach led to financial loss and damaged the company's reputation.

Risks of Weak Passwords

Using weak passwords can expose you to various risks, including:

  • Unauthorized access to personal and financial accounts.
  • Identity theft and financial loss.
  • Loss of data and privacy breaches.
  • Permanent damage to your online reputation.

Challenge Steps

Instructions: Use at least 12 characters, including a mix of upper and lower case letters, numbers, and special characters. Avoid using easily guessable information.

Instructions: Steer clear of easily guessable passwords like "password123" or your birthdate. Use password checkers to identify weak passwords.

Instructions: Consider using a password manager to generate and store complex passwords securely. This helps manage multiple accounts without repeating passwords.

Instructions: Activate two-factor authentication on your accounts to add an extra layer of security. This requires a second form of verification in addition to your password.

Instructions: Change your passwords regularly, ideally every 3 to 6 months. Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity.

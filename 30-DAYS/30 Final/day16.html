Improve Your Privacy by Resetting Your Advertising ID

Advertising IDs are unique identifiers used by companies to track your activity and deliver targeted ads. Resetting your Advertising ID can help protect your privacy by giving you a fresh start and reducing the accuracy of ad tracking.

What is an Advertising ID?

An Advertising ID is a unique identifier assigned to your device, which is used to track your online behavior for the purpose of showing you personalized ads. Here’s how it works:

  • Tracks your activity across apps and websites.
  • Used to create a profile of your interests for targeted advertising.
  • Linked to your device, not your personal identity.
  • Resetting it helps reduce the accuracy of ad tracking and gives you a fresh start.

Why Resetting Your Advertising ID is Important?

Failing to reset your Advertising ID can expose you to the following risks:

  • Extensive tracking across multiple apps and platforms.
  • Targeted ads based on a detailed profile created without your knowledge.
  • Loss of privacy and potential data breaches.
  • Reduced control over who has access to your data.

Steps to Disable or Reset Advertising ID

Instructions: Go to Settings > Privacy > General > Turn off “Let apps show me personalized ID”.

Instructions: Go to Settings > Privacy > Ads > Tap on "Reset Advertising ID". You can also toggle off "Opt out of Ads Personalization" for further privacy.

Instructions: Go to Settings > Privacy > Tracking > Toggle off "Allow Apps to Request to Track".
